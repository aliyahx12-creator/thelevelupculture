Why We Exist

Not a Gaming Tips Brand.
A Culture Brand.

This is not about kill streaks or rankings. The gap we're filling is bigger than that.

It's the one where Black women show up to gaming spaces and are made to feel like they arrived too late, don't belong, or have to earn their place.

It's the one where "gamer" has a default image — and it doesn't look like us. We're filling that gap. Gaming is the vehicle. Confidence, visibility, and personal growth are the destination.

Confidence
Built through action, not waiting until you feel ready. Every step forward is evidence you can handle more than you thought.
Visibility
Presence requires no permission. We center Black women — in gaming, in conversations, in culture — unapologetically.
Growth
Progress over perfection. Your timeline belongs to you — not the internet, not the industry, not anyone's expectations.
Representation
Representation changes culture. When you see yourself reflected — really reflected — something shifts. That's the work.
Community
We don't shrink here. A space where shared experience creates connection — and connection builds something lasting.

"I looked for this community before I built it. I didn't find it. So I built it."

Aliyah — Founder, The Level Up Culture

The Founder Story

Why I Built the Space
I Couldn't Find.

I started gaming at 31. I had no idea what I was doing. I felt out of place more than once.

I searched for a space where a Black woman who started gaming as a mom of four — with a full life already in motion — could belong without having to justify her presence.

I didn't find it. So I built it.

Somewhere in the process of learning, failing, trying again, and showing up in spaces that weren't built for me — I grew. Not as a gamer. As a person.

Gaming gave me confidence built through action. Evidence that I could handle more than I thought. That story deserves to be told. And it deserves a community around it.
